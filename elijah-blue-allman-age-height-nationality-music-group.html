Elijah Blue Allman Age, Height, Nationality, Music Group

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Elijah Blue Allman, professionally known as P. Exeter Blue, is an American musician with a captivating musical journey. Born on July 10, 1976, he is the son of iconic singer Cher and legendary musician Gregg Allman. As the half-brother of Chaz Bono, Delilah Allman, Michael Allman, Layla Allman, and Devon Allman, Elijah Blue has carved his path in the music industry.

Musical Career:

Elijah Blue Allman serves as the lead vocalist, guitarist, and lyricist for the industrial metal band Deadsy. The band, which experienced a hiatus in April 2007, featured other talented members such as Alec Püre on drums, Dr. Nner on synthesizer, Carlton Megalodon on Ztar, and Jens Funke on bass. Deadsy released two albums that showcased Elijah’s musical prowess – “Commencement” in 2002 and “Phantasmagore” on August 22, 2006. The thematic elements in Deadsy’s music draw inspiration from the 1955 film “This Island Earth.”

Elijah’s musical journey began at a young age when he received his first guitar from Gene Simmons, the bassist of the iconic rock band Kiss. At just 13 years old, he embarked on a tour as a guitarist with his mother, Cher. His early exposure to the music industry laid the foundation for his future endeavors.

In 1994, Elijah Blue Allman auditioned for the guitarist position in Nine Inch Nails, showcasing his versatility. Although he didn’t secure the spot, his audacious attempt marked a significant chapter in his musical exploration. Notably, he appeared as a guitarist in the music video for Cher’s hit song “If I Could Turn Back Time.”

Elijah’s collaboration extends beyond Deadsy, as he contributed to Thirty Seconds to Mars’ self-titled debut album. Additionally, he provided guest vocals for various bands, including Orgy, Coal Chamber, and Sugar Ray, showcasing his diverse musical interests.

Influence and Recognition:

Elijah Blue Allman’s musical style reflects a fusion of industrial metal elements with a unique artistic flair. His journey from touring with Cher to fronting Deadsy and collaborating with other prominent bands illustrates his passion and commitment to musical expression.
