Julie Andrews Has Made Some Smart Financial Decisions And They Paid Off

September 2024 · 5 minute read

Famed singer and actress Julie Andrews is nothing short of an entertainment legend. She has appeared in some of the most famous film musicals of all time, and is perhaps best remembered for her turns as Maria in The Sound of Music and the eponymous character in Mary Poppins. Younger audiences may know her better from The Princess Diaries, in which she starred alongside Anne Hathaway. Andrews has enjoyed massive success over the course of her decades-long career, and for her work has received just about every entertainment award imaginable, including an Academy Award for her work on Mary Poppins. Her unique voice has made her one of the most successful musical stars in the world, and has allowed her to continue working - often providing voice work for major animated films. She is one of the most beloved stars in the business.

But how has all this acclaim translated financially? How much is Julie Andrews's net worth? Read on to find out.

6 Julie Andrews Feels Lucky To Have Had Such A Successful Career

“I think you’re very lucky if you love what you do,” Andrews has said. “I think you’re very lucky if you’re capable of love as well, and God knows I’ve been fortunate enough to be able to do both.”

“[My mother] had always said to me, ‘Don’t you dare get a swollen head. Don’t you dare think you’re the best,’ and all of that. And I don’t think I ever would have, but it’s thanks to that drilling on her part that kept hammering it home. Over time, I got over that. The body of work that I’ve done speaks for itself. And you know, eventually I had the right [to be happy with it] and to stand up and say, ‘I’m a member of this wonderful community.’”

5 Julie Andrews Sued For Medical Malpractice After Surgery Damaged Her Voice

In the late 90s, Andrews was forced to undergo surgery for what were thought to be 'cancerous nodules' on her vocal cords. The removal was botched, however, leaving her voice permanently damaged. In 1999, she took on the doctors who had performed the procedure, suing for malpractice which has robbed her of her livelihood and caused intense emotional anguish. She settled for an undisclosed amount, and has spoken little on the subject since.

4 Julie Andrews Has Made An Art Out Of Diversifying

Andrews was devastated by the results of unsuccessful surgery on her vocal cords back in 1997 which left her unable to sing and perform as she previously had. Despite this terrible setback, however, the actress has found new means of artistic expression, and in recent years has entered into a variety of new ventures. She is author of a successful series of children’s books, and has also worked on Julie’s Greenroom, a Netflix show featuring ‘Ms Julie’ and a host of colorful puppets which has been a huge success on the platform.

Her voice has also been heard on Bridgerton, where she performs as the narrator, Lady Whistledown. Her performances in these new productions have been very well received, and introduced her to a new generation of viewers.

”The wonderful thing about all these new ways of making art,” says Andrews, “is that, despite losing my original voice, the one that brought me so much joy and gave me my way in the world, I have been able to find another.”

3 Learning About Financial Management Is Something Julie Andrews Has Worked At

It was only when Andrews reached her adult years that she began learning about money and how to manage it.

'My first agent was in charge of managing my work as a child performer,' she has stated. 'He held the purse strings into my early adulthood, and since I had been with him for so long I didn't question how my finances were managed.

'Later, I switched agents and made it a point to better educate myself. I am grateful to have had very good guidance over the years since, and the more I learned the better able I was to make sound decisions. A trusted agent or manager can make a very helpful difference.'

2 Julie Andrews Has Owned Some Stunning Homes

One of the most interesting properties the Mary Poppins actress has owned is her Brentwood home, which she parted with back in 2012 for a cool $2.649 million. The gated property, according to Celebrity Net Worth, features cathedral ceilings, an artist's studio, and four bedrooms. The outdoor area boasts a swimming pool and a spa. Julie and her husband originally purchased the property back in 1989 for $1.2 million.

1 So, How Much Is Julie Andrews Worth In Total?

It will be no surprise to read that Andrews is worth an impressive amount after all her hard work over the course of her career. Sources estimate that the 86-year-old is worth around $30 million. Now that's something to really sing about.
