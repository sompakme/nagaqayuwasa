Who is Lisa Abramowicz? Lisa Abramowicz Biography and Wiki

Lisa Abramowicz is an American journalist who currently co-hosts “Bloomberg Surveillance” from 7 to 9 a.m. on Bloomberg Radio and Bloomberg Television. She previously co-hosted “Bloomberg Markets” from 10 a.m. to noon EST on Bloomberg Radio and was a member of Bloomberg Television’s on-air markets desk. She also served as a columnist and reporter at Bloomberg focusing on fixed-income markets.

For almost two years, Lisa was a reporter for The Forum of Fargo-Moorhead in which she worked on breaking news stories. She covered and wrote features on crime, government, religion, and aging. During her time at North Dakota’s biggest newspaper, she won third prize in the North Dakota Newspaper Association’s 2002 contest for government reporting.

After her time in North Dakota, she returns to her native of New York and began working as a full-time reporter. At the Staten Island Advance, she was the health reporter for the daily newspaper’s news desk. She was also a part of the Glimmer Train publication in which she won third place for one of her short stories.

Lisa Abramowicz Career

Her career began in June 2004, when she was employed by Staten Island Advance as a health reporter. She served the company for about 3 years. Lisa worked as a health reporter for the daily newspaper’s news desk. She wrote breaking news and features about issues including hospital finances, epidemiology, personal health, and public programs.

She received an honorable mention for beat reporting in the 2007 New York State Associated Press Association Writing Contest among newspapers with circulations between 50,000 and 125,000. She left the company in August 2007.
In the year 2008, Lisa joined Glimmer Train as a writer.

She won third place in Glimmer Train’s May 2008 Short Story Award for New Writers competition. She was a senior writer at Harrison Scott Publication from September 2007 to September 2010.
