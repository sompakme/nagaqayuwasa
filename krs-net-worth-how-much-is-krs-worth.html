Early Life

KRS-One, whose real name is Lawrence Parker, was born on August 20, 1965, in the South Bronx, New York City. From a young age, KRS-One developed a deep passion for music and it became a driving force in his life. At the age of 16, he made the bold decision to leave home and pursue a career as an MC.

During this time, KRS-One found himself living in a homeless shelter in the South Bronx. While facing the challenges of his circumstances, he became immersed in the vibrant street art graffiti scene that thrived in the area. It was during these formative years that he adopted the alias KRS-One, a name that would soon become synonymous with the power and essence of true hip hop.

In a twist of fate, KRS-One’s life took a significant turn when he crossed paths with Scott Sterling, a dedicated youth counselor. Recognizing his talent and potential, Sterling became a mentor to KRS-One, igniting a creative partnership between DJ and MC that would ultimately lead to the formation of the influential hip hop group, Boogie Down Productions.

Boogie Down Productions and Stop the Violence Movement

Boogie Down Productions, formed in 1986, consisted of KRS-One, DJ Scott La Rock, and Derrick “D-Nice” Jones. The group released their debut album, “Criminal Minded,” in 1987, which gained critical acclaim.

Tragedy struck the group when Scott La Rock was fatally shot while mediating an argument. Despite the loss, Boogie Down Productions continued to release albums with a focus on social and political issues, becoming influential in the genre.

KRS-One also founded the Stop the Violence Movement, a hip hop group that aimed to raise awareness about violence in black communities through music.

Temple of Hip Hop

In the early 2000s, KRS-One founded the Temple of Hip Hop, an organization dedicated to promoting and preserving the culture of hip hop. The Temple of Hip Hop serves as a ministry, archive, school, and society that emphasizes the positive and empowering aspects of hip hop.

Every year, the Temple of Hip Hop celebrates Hip Hop Appreciation Week on the third week of May, encouraging fans to participate in events and activities that showcase the artistry and impact of hip hop.

Temple of Hip Hop Initiatives

The Temple of Hip Hop is involved in various initiatives that promote the education and awareness of hip hop culture. These initiatives include:

  • Hip Hop History Month: A month-long celebration in November dedicated to the history and evolution of hip hop.
  • Elementary Hip Hop Program: A program that introduces hip hop culture to elementary school students, teaching them about its history, elements, and positive values.
  • Community Outreach: The Temple of Hip Hop organizes outreach programs to engage with communities and spread awareness about hip hop culture’s transformative power.

“Hip hop is not just music, it’s a way of life. Through the Temple of Hip Hop, we aim to educate, inspire, and unite people through the power of this cultural movement.”– KRS-One

Hip Hop Appreciation Week

Hip Hop Appreciation Week, organized by the Temple of Hip Hop, is a week-long celebration of hip hop culture, art, and history. During this week, various events and activities take place, including:

  • Concerts and Performances: Live music shows featuring both established and emerging hip hop artists.
  • Art Exhibitions: Showcasing hip hop-inspired visual art, graffiti, and street art.
  • Workshops and Masterclasses: Educational sessions where aspiring artists can learn about the different elements of hip hop and develop their skills.
  • Panel Discussions and Lectures: Engaging conversations about the impact of hip hop on society, politics, and culture.

Hip Hop Appreciation Week aims to create a platform for artists, fans, and enthusiasts to come together, honor the legacy of hip hop, and inspire future generations.
