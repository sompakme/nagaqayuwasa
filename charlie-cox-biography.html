Charlie Cox is a renowned British actor who is best known for his role as Matt Murdock / Daredevil in Marvel’s Cinematic Universe (MCU) media franchise, having appeared in the Netflix series “Daredevil” (2015–2018), “The Defenders” (2017) and the film “Spider-Man: No Way Home” (2021). He is also widely recognized for his role as Tristan Thorn in the movie “Stardust” (2007). Further, he has also portrayed Jonathan Hellyer Jones in the 2014 film “The Theory of Everything” and Owen Sleater in the second and third seasons of HBO’s “Boardwalk Empire” (2011-2012). He starred for the first time in the psychological thriller film “Dot the I” in 2003 as Theo with Gael García Bernal. He was first seen on television in 2002 when he played Young Vicar in BBC’s legal drama series “Judge John Deed” in the Everyone’s Child episode. His first stage appearance was in 2005 in “Tis Pity she’s a Whore” as Giovanni at Southwark Playhouse.

What Ethnicity is Charlie Cox? Know About His Nationality, Family, and Education

Charlie was born on 15th December 1982 in England, the United Kingdom with the birth name of Charlie Thomas Cox. He is British by nationality and he is of mixed (English, Irish, and Scottish) ethnic background. He is the youngest of five children born to his mother, Patricia C. A. Tricia, and his father, Andrew Frederick Seaforth Cox. Thus, he has four siblings; a brother named Toby and three half-siblings; Emma (Step-Sister), Zoe (Step-Brother), Oliver (Step Brother from his father’s first marriage. Sagittarius is his Zodiac sign. He was raised Roman Catholic. But, he rarely practices religion. He celebrated his 39th birthday as of 2021. 

About his education, Charlie Cox did his schooling at Ashdown House School in Forest Row village in East Sussex, London. He also pursued studies at the Sherborne School in Sherborne market town, Dorset, London. He also studied theater at the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School in Bristol. 

Charlie Cox Career Timeline

  • Charlie Cox made his film debut in the year 2003 in the psychological thriller “Dot the i” when he was 18. 
  • Then, he was cast as Lorenzo in the 2004 Al Pacino-vehicle “The Merchant of Venice”. 
  • He next played the supporting roles in movies like the 2005 historical drama Casanova and the 2006 BBC sci-fi film “A for Andromeda”.
  • He then rose to fame after playing the main protagonist, Tristan Thorn, in the 2007 fantasy film “Stardust”.
  • He made his West End debut the following year in Harold Pinter’s “The Lover/The Collection”.
  • He was next seen in the 2008 film “Stone of Destiny” as well as in “Glorious 39”. 
  • In the year 2011, he played St. Josemaría Escrivá in the Roland Joffé film “There Be Dragons” and Ishmael in Encore’s “Moby Dick” miniseries.
  • In 2011, he appeared as Owen Sleater on the HBO series “Boardwalk Empire”. 
  • In 2013 he starred in the independent film “Hello Carter” and in the BBC Cold War thriller “Legacy”. 
  • At the end of 2013, production began on the film “The Theory of Everything”, where Cox portrays Jonathan Hellyer Jones.
  • Since 2015, he began playing the lead role as Matt Murdock in Marvel’s television series “Daredevil” and became the first non-American actor to play Matt Murdock/Daredevil. 
  • He is also the cast for the lead role in The Defenders. 
  • After “Daredevil” abruptly ended, he was seen in the West End production of Harold Pinter’s “Betrayal”.
  • In late 2018, he acted in the short film “The Knot”. 
  • In the fall of 2020, he began filming the RTE Dublin crime series “Kin”. 
  • In December 2021, Marvel Studios president Kevin Feige confirmed that Cox would reprise the role of Daredevil in the studio’s Marvel Cinematic Universe productions; his first project “remain[ed] to be seen” at that time. The first Marvel Studios production he appeared in was “Spider-Man: No Way Home” (2021).
