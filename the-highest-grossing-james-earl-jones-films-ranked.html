James Earl Jones is an American actor recognized for his loud and unique voice. He has had a long and successful career in the entertainment industry. Jones has appeared in a wide range of roles, both on screen and on stage, earning critical acclaim and numerous accolades throughout his career. Some of his notable works include providing the voice of Vader in the original Star Wars trilogy, his portrayal of Mufasa in Disney's animated film The Lion King, and his Tony Award-winning performance in the Broadway play Fences. Jones is considered one of his generation's greatest actors and has significantly impacted the entertainment industry. Jones has made much money throughout his career, totaling $40 million.

The Lion King (2019) -$1.6 billion

In The Lion King (2019), James Earl Jones resumed his role as Mufasa's voice. Mufasa is a wise and noble king of the Pride Lands and the father of Simba, the film's main character. Jones's deep and commanding voice brought Mufasa to life, just as he did in the original 1994 animated film. His performance added a sense of authority and warmth to the character, making Mufasa one of the most memorable and beloved characters in Disney's animated canon.

The Lion King (1994) -$763 million

In The Lion King (1994), James Earl Jones provided the voice for Mufasa, the wise and majestic king of the Pride Lands. He is the main character's father, Simba, and serves as a mentor and role model throughout the story. James Earl Jones's powerful and distinctive voice lent a commanding presence to the character, making Mufasa one of the most iconic and beloved characters in animated film history. His portrayal of Mufasa helped to bring depth, warmth, and wisdom to the character, leaving a lasting impression on his fans.

Star Wars: A New Hope (1977) -$775 million

In Star Wars: A New Hope (1977), Jones provided the voice for the iconic character Darth Vader. Although David Prowse physically portrayed Darth Vader on screen, James Earl Jones lent his deep and menacing voice to the Sith Lord. Darth Vader, aka Anakin Skywalker, is a prominent Star Wars figure and the original trilogy's principal antagonist. Jones's powerful voice and delivery added an air of intimidation and authority to the character, making Vader one of the most memorable villains in cinematic history.

Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back (1980) -$401.5 million

Jones repeats his role as the voice of Darth Vader. Like his previous role in Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope, Jones provided the deep, commanding voice that brought the iconic Sith lord to life. Vader, the main character in the Star Wars franchise, is a formidable adversary to the Rebel Alliance and a key figure in the overall story arc. Jones's voice performance continued to contribute to the menacing and authoritative presence of Darth Vader, solidifying the character's status as one of the most iconic villains in film history.

Conan the Barbarian (1982) -$300 million

In Conan, the Barbarian, James Earl Jones played the role of Thulsa Doom, the film's main antagonist. Thulsa Doom is a powerful sorcerer and cult leader that worships snakes. He is known for his charismatic and manipulative nature, using his dark magic and influence to control and exploit others. James Earl Jones brought a captivating and sinister presence to the character, portraying Thulsa Doom as a formidable and enigmatic figure. His performance added depth and intensity to the film, making Thulsa Doom one of the memorable adversaries in the Conan franchise.

Coming to America (1988) - $289 million

In the comedy film Coming to America (1988), James Earl Jones portrayed King Jaffe Joffer. King Jaffe Joffer is the father of Prince Akeem, the film's main protagonist, played by Eddie Murphy. As the wise and dignified ruler of the fictional African nation of Zamunda, King Jaffe Joffer plays a significant role in the story. Jones's performance showcased his ability to bring gravitas and humor to the character, creating a memorable portrayal of a regal and loving father figure. His presence and delivery added warmth and comedic moments to the film, contributing to its success.

Clear and Present Danger (1994) - $216 million

In the film Clear and Present Danger (1994), James Earl Jones reprised his role as Admiral James Greer. Admiral Greer is a high-ranking CIA official and mentor to the film's main character, Jack Ryan, portrayed by Harrison Ford. Greer plays a pivotal role in the story as he guides and supports Ryan in navigating a dangerous political conspiracy. James Earl Jones brought his authoritative and commanding presence to the role, portraying Greer as a seasoned and experienced intelligence operative. His performance added depth and credibility to the character, showcasing Greer's wisdom, leadership, and loyalty to his team.

The Hunt for Red October (1990) - $200.4 million

In The Hunt for Red October (1990), James Earl Jones portrayed the character Admiral James Greer. Admiral Greer is a high-ranking intelligence officer and mentor to the film's protagonist, Jack Ryan, played by Alec Baldwin. Greer plays a key role in the story, supporting and advising Ryan in pursuing a rogue Soviet submarine captain. Jones's portrayal of Admiral Greer showcased his authoritative presence and ability to command the screen. He brought a sense of wisdom, experience, and determination to the character, lending depth and credibility to the film. Jones's portrayal as Admiral Greer was well-received, contributing to the film's overall success, which grossed over $200 million.
