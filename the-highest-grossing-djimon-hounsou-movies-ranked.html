Djimon Hounsou was born in Benin, West Africa, in 1964. At thirteen, Hounsou moved to France, where he became a model. Hounsou later moved to the United States and continued his career as a model. Hounsou's breakthrough in the film industry came in 1997 when he appeared in Steven Spielberg's Amistad. He has also appeared in several other notable films, including Gladiator, Blood Diamond, and In America.

Hounsou is well-known for his activism, humanitarian work, and acting career. He has been involved with organizations such as Oxfam, the International Rescue Committee, and the Enough Project, which aim to improve conditions for people in poverty-stricken countries. Hounsou has received numerous awards and nominations for his acting. Here are the 8 highest-grossing Djimon Hounsou movies, ranked based on their worldwide box office figures.

Blood Diamond (2006) - $171 million

Edward Zwick directed Blood Diamond, starring Leonardo DiCaprio, Djimon Hounsou, and Jennifer Connelly. The film tells the story of the illegal diamond trade in Africa and the brutal civil war in Sierra Leone during the 1990s. The film follows Danny Archer (DiCaprio), a smuggler and former soldier searching for a rare pink diamond to sell to a buyer in South Africa. He joins forces with Solomon Vandy (Hounsou), a fisherman whose village was attacked by insurgents and whose family was captured and forced to work in diamond mines.

Constantine (2005) - $230 million

Constantine is a supernatural action film directed by Francis Lawrence and starring Keanu Reeves, Djimon Hounsou, and Rachel Weisz, among other stars. The film is based on the DC Comics character John Constantine, a chain-smoking demon hunter, and occult detective. Djimon Hounsou plays the role of Midnite in Constantine. Midnite is a powerful witch and owner of the Midnight Club, a supernatural nightclub in Los Angeles. Constantine received mixed reviews from critics and grossed $230 million worldwide.

Gladiator  - $460 million

Gladiator is a historical film directed by Ridley Scott and starring Russell Crowe, Joaquin Phoenix, and Djimon Hounsou, among others. Djimon Hounsou played the role of Juba in Gladiator. Juba was captured by Roman soldiers and sold into slavery. He becomes a gladiator and forms a close friendship with Maximus (Russell Crowe), who saves his life during a battle in the arena. The film was a commercial success, grossing $460 million worldwide.

How to Train Your Dragon 2 (2014) - $621 million

How to Train Your Dragon 2 is an animated action-fantasy film directed by Dean DeBlois and produced by DreamWorks Animation. Djimon Hounsou voices the character of Drago Bludvist in this film. Drago is the film's main antagonist, a ruthless dragon hunter who seeks to control all dragons and use them as his army to conquer the world.

Guardians of the Galaxy (2014) - $773 million

The film was directed by James Gunn and stars Chris Pratt, Zoe Saldana, Djimon Hounsou, Dave Bautista, Vin Diesel, and Bradley Cooper. Hounsou plays the role of Korath the Pursuer in Guardians of the Galaxy (2014). Korath is a member of the Kree race and serves as one of the primary henchmen of the film's main villain, Ronan the Accuser

Aquaman (2018) - $1.148 billion

Aquaman is a superhero film released in 2018, directed by James Wan and produced by Warner Bros. Pictures. The movie stars Jason Momoa as Arthur Curry, a half-human who is the rightful heir to the throne. Djimon Hounsou plays the character King Ricou, one of the kings of the Fisherman Kingdom.

Furious 7 (2015) - $1.516 billion

Furious 7 is the seventh edition of the Fast and Furious film series, released in 2015. The film is written by Chris Morgan and directed by James Wan. It stars Vin Diesel, Paul Walker, Dwayne Johnson, Michelle Rodriguez, Jordana Brewster, Tyrese Gibson, Djimon Hounsou, Ludacris, and Jason Statham. Djimon Hounsou played the role of Mose Jakande, a ruthless mercenary who works for the film's main antagonist, Deckard Shaw (played by Jason Statham). The film was a commercial success grossing $1.516 billion worldwide.
