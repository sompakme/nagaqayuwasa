Inside Tiger Woods' Career

Since he first emerged on the golf scene in the late 1990s, Tiger Woods has captivated audiences with his remarkable talent and steely focus. Most sports fans would agree that he has had a stellar career and has left a mark in the golfing world that few can surpass.

In his lengthy career, Woods has achieved countless milestones. One of his most notable accolades include winning 15 major championships. He has also had several victories at distinguished events like the U.S. Open, The Open Championship, and the Masters, all of which have added to the famous golfer's allure.

Tiger Woods' Relationship Scandals And Injuries

Despite his fame and fortune, Woods' path to stardom has had its share of setbacks. The golfer has experienced countless injuries throughout his career, which have resulted in several missed games.

One of Tiger Woods' most significant injuries occurred after he was in a severe car crash. The impact was so bad that first responders had to extract Woods' from the vehicle. The story made headline news, with initial reports fearing that the golfer had died.

Despite the seriousness of the wreck, Woods luckily survived with injuries to his ankle and two leg fractures, which significantly impacted his golfing career for a time. In recent years, Woods has been afflicted with plantar fasciitis in his right foot and, as a result, has had to pull out from several games.

Woods' personal life has also taken center stage on numerous occasions. The golfer's reputation had been nearly spotless until his messy public split with his first wife and mother of his two children, Elin Nordegren.

However, the media revealed that Woods had cheated on his wife with multiple women. He received so much bad publicity from his actions that Tiger Woods was compelled to publicly apologize to his family, fans, and the media.
